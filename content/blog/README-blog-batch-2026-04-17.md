## Evidence and caveats
### Stronger areas
- ApoB as a useful marker of atherogenic particle burden
- Lp(a) as an inherited cardiovascular risk marker often worth measuring at least once
- insulin resistance often preceding abnormal fasting glucose
- postprandial glucose and CGM as useful additional context in selected patients
- resistance training, protein, sleep and cardiorespiratory fitness as major metabolic health levers

### Claims that should receive clinician review before publication
1. Any mention of ideal thresholds or exact targets if you want numerically precise public-facing guidance.
2. Any therapeutic language around future or emerging Lp(a)-lowering drugs.
3. Ferritin framing in the fatigue article, especially if you want to preserve the “one lab test” hook while avoiding oversimplification.
4. Any suggestion that biological age testing is clinically actionable beyond motivational/supportive use.
5. Any broad statements implying CGM should be used routinely in all non-diabetic patients.

## Editorial notes
- UK spelling is used throughout.
- Tone is intentionally clear and patient-friendly.
- The wording avoids direct diagnosis and avoids promising outcomes.
- The content is suitable for lead-gen blog use but should still be reviewed by a clinician before publication, especially where risk interpretation or treatment implications are concerned.
