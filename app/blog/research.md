# Fast Insulin: The Missing Link in Longevity

Fasting insulin—measured in mIU/L—is the most sensitive marker for early metabolic dysfunction. While standard glucose checks (measured in mmol/L [mg/dL]) often remain in the 'normal' range for decades, insulin is already silently rising as the body attempts to maintain homeostasis.

For longevity-focused health, the physiological sweet spot for fasting insulin is typically below 5–7 mIU/L. Levels above this indicate hyperinsulinemia, a state that accelerates glycation and blunts the body's ability to enter ketosis or utilize fat stores for energy. By prioritizing fasting insulin as a primary metric, we move from reactive medicine to a model of proactive metabolic preservation.

# ApoB vs. LDL: The True Cardiovascular Risk Marker

ApoB (Apolipoprotein B) is the protein backbone for every atherogenic lipoprotein particle in your blood. Conventional medicine relies on LDL-C (measured in mmol/L [mg/dL]), which is merely an estimation of the cholesterol *contained within* those particles. 

ApoB, however, measures the actual particle *count*. Because cardiovascular disease is a function of the number of particles that penetrate the arterial wall, ApoB is a significantly more robust predictor of risk than LDL-C. High ApoB levels—even when LDL-C appears "average"—indicate a higher likelihood of atherosclerosis. Our goal at Veridian is to optimize ApoB as a core pillar of vascular longevity.

# Reversing Metabolic Syndrome in 12 Weeks

Metabolic syndrome is a cluster of conditions—high blood pressure (mmHg), elevated blood sugar, excess waist circumference (cm [in]), and abnormal cholesterol levels—that collectively increase the risk of heart disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes. 

Reversing this process is not merely about weight loss; it is about restoring insulin sensitivity. Our 12-week protocol focuses on a staged approach: 
1. Elimination of ultra-processed carbohydrates that trigger insulin spikes.
2. Introduction of resistance training to increase skeletal muscle glucose uptake.
3. Time-restricted feeding to allow endogenous insulin levels to fall. 

This process isn't just restorative; it is protective, providing the metabolic infrastructure necessary for decades of healthy function.
